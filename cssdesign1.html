Safety Measures Should Be Taken

In coordination with WHO Country Office for Sri Lanka, the Ministry of Health and Local Medical Services continues to track the situation closely and to improve preparedness and response efforts. We are work closely with main collaborators to fight COVID-19 efficiently and successfully. More than six months since the pandemic, Sri Lanka has made considerable strides in managing the transmission of COVID-19, but the challenge has not yet stopped. We will draw on the gains made to keep ourselves, our loved ones, and our societies safe, stable and prosperous.


Rumors and disinformation can make communication and life-saving simpler. They advise everyone to follow high-quality, reliable sources of information that will enable us to remain healthy. Solidarity is one of our greatest weapons. We all have a part to play; it's not an option between life and livelihood.

To Prevent COVID-19, Follow These 9 steps:

  • Wash your hands thoroughly and frequently.
  • Avoid touching your mouth, nose , and eyes.
  • Cover your mouth as you cough or sneeze with forearm or tissue (if you use a towel, quickly dispose of it in a closed bin and wash your hands or use a hand sanitizer).
  • Avoid crowded places.
  • Practice physical distancing, seek to keep at least 1m away from others.
  • Clean and disinfect surfaces regularly
  • Stay at home if you're not feeling well (fever and respiratory symptoms).
  • When you have signs (fever, cough, and trouble breathing) seek medical attention, first by phone.
  • Keep up-to-date on the latest information from verified outlets such as MoH and WHO.
